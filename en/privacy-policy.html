SwipeClear Privacy Policy

SwipeClear (“the App”) is developed by aicoconfs. We value your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how the App accesses, processes, stores, and protects your data.

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, please contact us at: aicoconfs@gmail.com

1. Scope and Consent

This Privacy Policy applies to the Android version of the App published on the Google Play Store.

By downloading, installing, or using the App, you acknowledge and agree to the data practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with this Privacy Policy, please stop using the App and uninstall it from your device.

2. Core Features and Local Processing Notice

The App is a photo organization tool. Its core features include photo swiping and organization, photo browsing, similar photo grouping, organization progress tracking, and local photo library management.

The App’s main photo processing features are performed locally on your device, including photo display, organization status tracking, similar photo comparison, and similar group caching.

The App does not upload your actual photo files to our servers. We do not provide cloud photo backup, remote photo analysis, or server-side photo recognition services.

3. Data Access, Collection, and Use

3.1 Locally Stored Data

To provide photo organization, similar photo grouping, progress tracking, and user preference features, the App stores certain data locally on your device. This data is primarily stored in the App’s private storage area using the local database Isar and SharedPreferences.

The App may locally store the following types of data:

  • Photo asset identification data: Such as photo Asset IDs, creation time, modification time, media type, width, and height, used for displaying, sorting, filtering, and managing photos.
  • Photo organization status: Such as whether a photo has been viewed, organized, marked as favorite, and related timestamps.
  • Similar photo analysis data: Such as locally generated similarity fingerprints, hash features, similar group IDs, grouping results, analysis time, and cache status derived from photos or thumbnails.
  • User preferences: Such as theme mode, sound settings, filter options, display preferences, and other local settings.
  • Local usage statistics: Such as organization progress, number of organized photos, and number of viewed photos, used to display in-app progress and statistics.

The data listed above is used only for local App functionality and is not actively uploaded by us to our servers.

3.2 Similar Photo Feature Data Processing

The App’s similar photo feature analyzes photos or photo thumbnails locally on your device to generate similarity fingerprints and grouping results. This data is used to identify photos that may be visually similar and help you organize your photo library more efficiently.

Similar photo analysis data is derived data generated from your photos. It may include local photo identifiers, similarity features, grouping information, and analysis time, but it does not include complete photo files that can directly restore the original photo content.

The App may store similar photo analysis results in the local database to avoid re-analyzing all photos every time the App is opened, improving performance and user experience.

3.3 Third-Party Advertising Data

The App uses the Google Mobile Ads SDK (AdMob) to provide advertising services. To load, display, measure, and improve ads, the Google Mobile Ads SDK may collect or process certain data, such as:

  • Device identifiers: Such as the Android Advertising ID (AAID).
  • Technical data: Such as IP address, device information, operating system version, network status, and ad request information.
  • Ad interaction data: Such as ad impressions, clicks, views, and interaction records.

This type of data is processed by the Google Mobile Ads SDK according to its service mechanisms and may be transmitted to Google or its partner servers. For more information about Google’s data practices, please refer to Google’s Privacy Policy:

https://policies.google.com/privacy

4. Permission Usage

To ensure that the core features work properly, the App may request the following Android system permissions depending on your Android version and device settings:

  • READ_MEDIA_IMAGES (Android 13 and above): Used to read and display photos from your device’s photo library.
  • READ_EXTERNAL_STORAGE (older Android versions): Used to read photos from your device’s photo library on older Android versions.
  • WRITE_EXTERNAL_STORAGE (older Android versions, where applicable): Used for photo deletion or media management operations on older Android versions.
  • Photo and video selection permission or partial photo access permission, depending on the Android version: If your system provides options such as “Allow access to selected photos only,” the App will access photos only within the scope you authorize.
  • INTERNET: Used by the Google Mobile Ads SDK to load and display ads.
  • ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE: Used to check network status to assist with ad loading and display.

The App does not require additional storage permission to use Isar or SharedPreferences for local data storage, because this data is stored in the App’s private storage area.

5. Data We Do Not Collect

The App follows a privacy-by-design approach. We do not actively collect the following data:

  • Direct personal identifiers such as your name, phone number, or email address.
  • Precise GPS location data or real-time location information.
  • Biometric data.
  • Contacts, SMS messages, call logs, or calendar data.
  • Microphone recordings.
  • Real-time camera captures.
  • Complete photo files for cloud backup or remote analysis purposes.

Please note that the Google Mobile Ads SDK may process device, network, and ad interaction data according to its advertising service mechanisms. Such data processing is governed by Google’s relevant policies.

6. Data Retention and Deletion

6.1 Local Data Retention

As long as the App remains installed on your device, the App’s local data may be retained, including organization status, favorite markers, similar photo fingerprints, similar grouping results, settings, and local statistics.

This data is used to maintain App functionality, such as avoiding repeated similar photo analysis, preserving organization progress, and providing a consistent user experience.

6.2 Deleting Local Data

You can delete local data stored by the App through the following methods:

  • Use the “Clear app data” option in Android system settings.
  • Uninstall the App.
  • If the App provides features such as clearing cache, resetting progress, or similar controls, you may use those features to delete part of the local data.

After uninstalling the App, local data stored in the App’s private storage area is generally removed by the system.

6.3 Account Deletion

The App does not provide or require user accounts. Therefore, we do not store any account data about you on our servers, and you do not need to submit an account deletion request.

If you wish to delete data related to the App, you can clear the App data through Android system settings or uninstall the App.

7. Data Sharing and Third-Party Services

Except for advertising-related data required by the Google Mobile Ads SDK, the App does not actively provide your photos, photo organization status, similar photo fingerprints, similar grouping results, or other local data to third parties.

The third-party service used by the App includes:

  • Google Mobile Ads SDK (AdMob): Used to load and display ads.

Google may process relevant data according to its Privacy Policy and advertising policies. You may visit Google’s privacy and ad settings pages for more information.

8. International Privacy Rights

We respect users’ privacy rights and strive to comply with applicable data protection laws.

8.1 Users in the European Economic Area

If you are located in the European Economic Area, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, restrict the processing of, or object to the processing of your personal data.

Because the App’s main functional data is stored locally on your device, you can delete local data through Android system settings, by clearing App data, or by uninstalling the App.

8.2 California Users

If you are a California resident, you may have rights to know, access, delete, and opt out of certain sharing or sale of personal information.

The App does not sell your photo files, similar photo analysis data, or local organization data to third parties. The Google Mobile Ads SDK may process advertising-related data according to its advertising service mechanisms. Please refer to Google’s Privacy Policy and ad settings for more information.

9. Children’s Privacy

The App is not specifically designed for children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

If you are a parent or guardian and believe that a child has provided personal information to us without consent, please contact us at aicoconfs@gmail.com. We will take reasonable steps to assist with the matter.

10. Data Security

The App’s local data is stored in the App’s private storage area on your device, which is generally not directly accessible by other apps.

However, no method of data storage or transmission can be guaranteed to be 100% secure. Please protect your device, system account, and screen lock settings to reduce the risk of unauthorized access.

11. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in App features, practices, third-party services, or legal requirements.

If this Privacy Policy is materially changed, we will update the date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically to stay informed about how we protect your data.

12. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or our data practices, please contact us at:

aicoconfs@gmail.com
